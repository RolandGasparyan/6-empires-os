# Business Operating System

## Purpose
Define the business-layer operating model for finance, accounting, CRM, HR, recruiting, marketing, sales, legal, operations, partnerships, investors, and licensing.

## Ownership
Founder owns business authority. Function leads own domain operations. Platform and knowledge teams enable instrumentation and automation.

## Inputs and outputs
**Inputs:** financial events, customer data, hiring plans, legal obligations, partnership activity, investor requirements, platform telemetry.
**Outputs:** business workflows, compliance records, revenue reporting, pipeline status, hiring actions, operating dashboards.

## Approval model
Routine analysis may be automatic. Financial commitments, legal decisions, investor disclosures, and policy exceptions require founder approval.

## Operational risks
- disconnected business data from platform reality
- compliance drift
- automation crossing legal or financial approval boundaries
- weak ownership between business and technical systems

## Observability requirements
Track revenue flow, pipeline health, hiring status, partnership lifecycle, compliance exceptions, investor reporting cadence, and business-system data freshness.

## Future evolution path
Integrate business operations into the same knowledge, approval, and observability fabric as the engineering platform.
