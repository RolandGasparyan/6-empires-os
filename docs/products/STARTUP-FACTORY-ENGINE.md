# Startup Factory Engine

## Purpose
Define the repeatable venture-creation pipeline that turns ideas into validated, deployed, measured, and scalable startups.

## Ownership
Founder owns venture portfolio direction. Product, research, architecture, design, engineering, marketing, and analytics functions own their stage outcomes.

## Inputs and outputs
**Inputs:** ideas, market research, strategic priorities, reusable platform capabilities, brand and design constraints.  
**Outputs:** validated ventures, architecture packages, MVPs, launches, analytics loops, scale plans.

## Dependencies
- `docs/business/BUSINESS-OPERATING-SYSTEM.md`
- `docs/knowledge/KNOWLEDGE-RAINFOREST-ENGINE.md`
- `docs/roadmap/ENTERPRISE-ROADMAP.md`

## Venture pipeline
Idea → Validation → Research → Architecture → Brand → Design → MVP → Development → Testing → Deployment → Marketing → Analytics → Scale

## Standard controls
Every startup must define:
- problem and market thesis
- validation evidence
- architecture fit with shared platform assets
- launch metrics
- operational owner
- shutdown or pivot criteria

## Approval model
Idea exploration may be automatic. Venture commitment, brand launch, production rollout, and material budget allocation require founder approval.

## Operational risks
- parallel venture sprawl without shared platform reuse
- weak validation entering build
- analytics gaps preventing fast pivots
- brand, product, and technical systems diverging

## Observability requirements
Track venture funnel conversion, stage duration, dependency bottlenecks, launch metrics, retention signals, and portfolio risk concentration.

## Future evolution path
Turn the startup factory into a reusable internal operating pipeline backed by templates, automation, and shared knowledge primitives.
